Recruitment Process

Recruiting starts with a need of a professional to do a specific kind a job. After this, the job description and responsibilities are compiled together. Recruiting usually means selecting from a big number of resumes, those who match the necessary skills and experience, but they are not crucial and play just one role in the whole process of first seeing the resume to the actual placement and the phrase “Thank you Mr..., we'll see you tomorrow at the office”. At Intersog we do our best to review all resumes that you send us, and make sure that your skills, experience and motivation meet with ours in the best way and lead to a fruitful partnership. Below are listed the stages of our recruitment process:

  1. Reviewing the resume. On this stage we do the initial screening and see if a candidate's listed skills and experience match with required.
  2. Phone screen interview with the HR Manager. Getting to know the candidate.
  3. In person interview with the team leader.
  4. According to our policy we are do a background check for security reasons. Thus, we ask each candidate to present the contact information of former employers.
  5. Offer made.

How to prepare for an interview

Interview is one of the most important stages in your hiring process. This is your chance to present yourself and amaze the Hiring Manager with your skills and personality and, most importantly, get placed. In our company we follow the best strategies of an interviewing process, we respect each candidate and try to ease the stress of a screening process itself. Here are a few preparation tips which should help you to do your best on any interview.

  1. First of all, plan ahead. Make a research about the company and the position if possible.
  2. Review your work experience. Be ready to speak about your past career success and support your information with facts. If you have, bring a portfolio with your work results or presentation, fact usually speak for themselves.
  3. Practice. Think of the answers to possible questions to help you sound confident on the interview. Try to keep your answers to the information your new employer will want to know.
  4. Plan ahead your interview outfit. Make sure your clothes are washed and ironed, your shoes are polished and your hair and nails and well groomed. You need to make an impression even before you start speaking.
  5. Turn off your cell phone. Don't stare at the interviewer, act normally.
  6. Remember that your personality can play a crucial role on the selection process. Be nice and friendly to everybody, don't forget to smile.
  7. Encourage the interviewer to share more information about the company, show your interest.
